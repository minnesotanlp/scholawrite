\subsection{Combinations of Two Styles}\label{sec:two_style}
We first fine-tune RL models for all four possible combinations of sentiment and formality. We choose these two styles since their respective discriminators have the highest F1 scores (Table~\ref{table:discrims}).  We use the GradWt reward formulation, as it was the most successful reward formulation based on the evaluation in Section~\ref{sec:reward_eval}. We consider the efficacy of generations from the resulting models, as well as the results of other approaches for multi-style controlled generations. For the results of these two-style combinations, see Table~\ref{tab:precision_base-rl-pplm}. 
















\subsection{Combinations of Three Styles}\label{sec:three_styles}
We further extend the reward formulation to control for three target styles to assess the scalability of our approach as the number of target styles increases. We maintain the initial two style dimensions explored earlier, sentiment and formality. This decision is motivated in part by the high performance of these style discriminators, and in part by a desire to understand to what extent adding a third style control influences the ability to control these dimensions relative to only two styles. 

We add third style dimensions based on our other discriminators for toxicity, irony, and emotion.  We limit our three style models to four distinct three-style combinations due to computational constraints (fully exploring the three-style combination space provided by all of our discriminators would require fine-tuning $212$ models, where each fine-tuning procedure needs to load the language model coupled with three discriminators).
