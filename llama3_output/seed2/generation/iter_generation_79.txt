  % \noindent \textbf{Impact of sampling strategy}:
  % Ego graph sampling \cite{spreen1994sampling} involves selecting a central node and its direct connections to form a subgraph. The chosen ego biases this method; for example, an unusually connected ego affects the subgraph's representativeness. Forest Fire sampling \cite{leskovec2006sampling}, in contrast, uses a spread-like mechanism starting from a random node to select network portions. Its randomness means subgraphs vary across instances, influenced by parameters like burning probabilities, and can be seen as a more representative sample of the entire network, especially for large, diverse networks. Both methods have their uses, and the choice of which to use depends on the specific goals of the application. We expect that the Forest Fire sampling method would be more likely to provide a sample representative of the entire network, especially with large and diverse networks like \textsc{Pubmed}.
