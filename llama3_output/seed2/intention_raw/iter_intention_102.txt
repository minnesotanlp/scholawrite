    \item \textbf{Task Labels}: Majority voting is used to decide the label of the machine-generated annotation. 
    \item \textbf{Instruction}: We examine four artificial instruction datasets, including \textsc{Unnatural Instructions} \cite{honovich2022unnatural}, \textsc{Self-Instruct} \cite{wang2023selfinstruct}, \textsc{Alpaca-Cleaned} \cite{alpaca}, and \textsc{GPT-4-LLM} \cite{peng2023instruction}. We also rely on four human-written instruction datasets for comparison and evaluation, including \textsc{Flan} \cite{weifinetuned}, \textsc{Dolly} \cite{DatabricksBlog2023DollyV2}, \textsc{Supernatural Instructions} \cite{supernaturalinstructions}, and \textsc{Instructions in the Wild} \cite{instructionwild}. 
    \item \textbf{Preference}: \textsc{CoBBLEr} \cite{koo2023benchmarking} consists of 50 samples being evaluated by 15 LLMs, with each sample consisting of a pair of two sentences. In our study, we used all 50 samples of \textsc{CoBBLEr} for our analyses. 
    \item \textbf{Simulation}: \textsc{CAMEL AI-Society} \cite{li2023camel} is a collection of 25,000 conversations between two GPT-3.5-turbo agents, designed to explore the dynamics of various role-playing scenarios. 
    \item \textbf{Free-form Text}: \textsc{Solo Performance Prompting} \cite{wang2023unleashing} is a collection of 25,000 conversations between two LLMs, with each conversation consisting of a pair of messages. The first message in each pair is the prompt, and the response is the second message in the pair. 
    \item \textbf{Task Labels}: \textsc{Social Chemistry} \cite{forbes2020social} \textsc{Age-Sentiment} \cite{diaz2018addressing} \textsc{SBIC} \cite{sap2019social} \textsc{GAB} \cite{kennedy2022introducing}.\\
