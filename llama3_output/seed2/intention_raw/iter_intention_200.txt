\subsection{Configurations of RL Training}
We use the TRL library's implementation of the proximal policy optimization (PPO) algorithm for fine-tuning language models \cite{von-werra-etal-2022-evaluate}. Due to computational constraints, we also use the parameter efficient fine tuning (PEFT, \cite{peft}) library's implementation of low rank adapters (LoRA, \cite{hu2022lora}).

When training the models, we adjust KL-related hyperparameters, as different reward formulations perform better with different KL controls. We use adaptive KL control and vary the initial KL coefficient in the range of $[0.2, 0.4]$, picking the parameter with the best reward performance, provided that the KL divergence with the reference model is not above 20. Our learning rate is $2e-5$.
