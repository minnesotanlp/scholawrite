\paragraph{Formality } 
We first explore how LLM-generated and human-written texts differ concerning high-level attributes, especially formality, hypothesizing that LLMs may not mirror humans very well in social and emotional contexts. We use the formality scorer from \cite{rao2018dear} on every text generated by humans and machines. For machine-generated texts, we also employ a token-level formality scorer developed by \cite{Babakov2023Formality} that is finetuned on a dataset for formality style transfer task, \textsc{GYAFC} \citep{rao-tetreault-2018-dear}. The formality scorer is based on the idea that formality is a function of text style, and it uses a  for every text generated by humans and machines.
