– For the sentiment preference in \textsc{P2C}, we observe that GPT-3 shows a strong tendency to prefer their preferences when the sentence with a higher entailment score of each sentence pair is the one that wins. In contrast, humans show only a minor correlation and can be tricked easily during the entailment-based preference.

– For the sentiment preferences in \textsc{Cobbler}, we observe that GPT-3 and humans show the same behavior in terms of correlation with GPT-3's and humans' lexicon-based preferences, respectively. Also, GPT-3's preferences in this dataset are more related to the entailment of the sentence that wins the entailment-based preference than humans' preferences do. 

– For the QA preferences in \textsc{cobbler}, we observe that GPT-4's preferences are more related to the entailment of the sentence that wins the entailment-based preference than humans' preferences do. Also, GPT-4's preferences on the QA samples are more related to the entailment of the sentence that wins the entailment-based preference than humans' preferences do.
