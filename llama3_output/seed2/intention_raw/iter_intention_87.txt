%\textbf{Acknowledge the rise of LLMs}
Large language models (LLMs) like ChatGPT \cite{} and now GPT4 \cite{bubeck2023sparks} present massive context-aware knowledge and superior semantic comprehension capability through pre-training on large-scale text corpora \cite{petroni2019language}. This knowledge achieved from pre-training has led to a surge of revolutions for downstream NLP tasks \cite{zhao2023survey}. Large Language Models (LLMs) have also expanded their applications beyond language processing into domains like robotics \cite{huang2022towards}, planning \cite{andreas2022language}, theory-of-mind reasoning \cite{adhikari2020learning}, structured commonsense reasoning \cite{madaan2022language}, and multi-hop question answering. \textbf{Our contributions}: Specifically, the contributions of our work are the following:

\begin{itemize}
  \item  An extensive study of graph-structured data and associated prompts for use in LLMs.
  \item Exploring the impact of graph structure, sampling methods, and no. of hops on node prediction performance
  \item A new graph benchmark (GraphTMI) with a Task hierarchy consisting of graphs and associated encoding modalities to aid the community in studying the effects of graph structure on LLM prompting further.
