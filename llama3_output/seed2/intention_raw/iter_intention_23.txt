For the first order effects, we observe that the synthetic datasets (i.e., \textsc{Unnatural Instructions} and \textsc{Self-Instruct}) have more hallucinations in their closed-QA type instructions than the human-generated counterparts. This makes sense, because instruction writers tend to focus on the specific inputs and outputs that are part of the task, while the writer may not think through the instruction's full context.
