\paragraph{Conclusion} 
In addition to using the  application to compose Related Work sections, the authors of this study also used it to draft the introduction of the  paper that was presented at the 2017 ACL conference\footnote{The final version of this paper is available in the ACL anthology.}. It is worth noting that the  application was used in an educational setting, where the intention was to develop a collaborative writing tool. However, in our study, we did not use the application to generate text independently of human input. The  application was used in a group setting, with multiple users collaborating on a single document. We acknowledge that this setup is not a traditional use of the tool, but rather a novel application. We intended to explore its potential benefits and pitfalls, rather than its use as a direct writing tool. However, it's important to acknowledge that our study is focused on the collaborative aspect of writing, rather than the use of specific tools for authoring. 

It's also important to highlight that our study evaluated the outputs of the GPT-4 with a focus on their textual similarity, rather than on the specific conditions of their generation. This choice was made to understand the relative quality of the generated texts, without being overly constrained by the conditions of use. We recognize that the use of GPT-4 in a production setting would likely involve more consideration of factors like semantic meanings, and that the examples generated by GPT-4 should be seen as a sample of the kind of work that could be produced in a collaborative setting. Our aim was not to make a comprehensive evaluation of GPT-4's quality, but rather to perform a simple comparison of its outputs with human-written texts. We acknowledge that this approach, while pragmatic, has the potential to introduce unintended biases and should be approached with caution.
