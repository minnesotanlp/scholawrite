\subsection{Combinations of Two Styles}\label{sec:two_style}
We first fine-tune RL models for all four possible combinations of sentiment and formality. We choose these two styles since their respective discriminators have the highest F1 scores (Table~\ref{table:discrims}).  We use the GradWt reward formulation, as it was the most successful reward formulation based on the evaluation in Section~\ref{sec:reward_eval}. We consider the efficacy of generations from the resulting models, as well as the results of other approaches for multi-style controlled generations. \paragraph{Comparison with PPLM}
We extend PPLM to combine feedback from multiple discriminators. PPLM has weaker control when combining two attributes than when using one, indicating that the approach may suffer more from conflicting objectives than a reinforcement learning approach. \paragraph{Comparison with Zero-Shot GPT-4}
We focus on a basic zero-shot prompting approach to probe GPT-4 performance on controlled style generations. We chose zero-shot prompting as we are interested in scenarios with high data-scarcity, i.e. those in which the target style combination text is not necessarily available. Note that while few-shot prompting can out-perform zero-shot prompting; this is not necessarily the case -- recent work suggests that GPT-4's performance can be equivalent or even deteriorate in a few-shot setting, e.g. \citet{coyne2023analyzing}.
