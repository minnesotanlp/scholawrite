\section{Introduction}
\rubric{Hook: } The rapid acceleration of knowledge creation is a defining feature of modern research. Between 1952 and 2020, the corpus of scientific literature has expanded at a growth rate of 5.08\%, doubling every 14 years \cite{Bornmann2021}. Some fields are growing even more rapidly; \citet{Krenn2023} estimate a doubling rate of almost two years for machine learning and artificial intelligence papers between 2022 and 2023. Global crises are also associated with rapid literature growth. Climate change publication has experienced a doubling rate of every 5-6 years from 1980 to 2014 \cite{10.1371/journal.pone.0160393}, and \citet{Chen_Allot_Leaman_Wei_Aghaarabi_Guerrerio_Xu_Lu_2022} reported a COVID-19 literature growth rate of approximately 10,000 articles per month from the Spring of 2020 to the Summer of 2022. 

\rubric{Context: } In response to this growth rate, numerous AI-assisted scholarly applications have been developed to aid different stages of the research process, including literature discovery, reading interfaces, and writing tools (see Table \ref{tab:applications} for a list of such publicly available tools and their features). In addition to using assistive AI-based tools, scholars are using ChatGPT directly in the academic writing process. For example, \citet{Savelka2023Can} found 1,226 publications between 2022 and 2023 where the authors acknowledged using ChatGPT to write their paper.

Given the increased use of AI-assisted scholarly writing and literature review tools, it is important to study the ways in which researchers interact with AI-assisted scholarly interfaces. This interaction is critical because the act of writing, far from being a mere procedural task to be completed at the end of the research process, is a dynamic aspect of academic inquiry and an important part of the writer's intellectual journey.
