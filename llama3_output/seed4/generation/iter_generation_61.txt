However, there is a notable misalignment between human preferences and LLM preferences in question-answering tasks \cite{zheng2023judging}, with LLMs showing a stronger tendency to favor majority opinions over minority ones \cite{li2023synthetic}. This disparity in LLMs' understanding of minority opinions, particularly in subjective contexts, poses a challenge for the models' effectiveness in mirroring human behavior. As LLMs become more widespread, their reliance on superficial cues such as lexical features and semantic similarities may become more pronounced, leading to a decreased ability to capture the complexity and nuanced understanding found in human-generated content.
