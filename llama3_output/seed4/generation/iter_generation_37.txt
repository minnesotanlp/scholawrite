\item \textbf{Instruction}: We use artificial data for the instruction fine-tuning, which we found is useful in combining a variety of instruction types and domains. We can see the potential of LLM-generated data in instructional data scarcity tasks. 
\item \textbf{Preference}: We use LLM-generated data for preference fine-tuning, which we found improves the quality of LLM-generated preferences. We also observe that LLM preferences are correlated with the local information of sentences, rather than the entire context, when compared to human preferences. 
\item \textbf{Simulation}:  We use LLM-generated data for role-flipping, which we found has a positive correlation with the number of roles flipped. We also observe that role-flipping is more likely to happen in long conversations with more messages.
