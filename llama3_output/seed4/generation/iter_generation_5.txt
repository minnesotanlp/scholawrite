First, we check how the different generation settings affect the detection performance (Table \ref{tab:overall_accuracy_ten_page_stories}). As expected the models perform better when longer texts are generated. Also, when the first and last sentences of the human-written texts are additionally provided in the prompt (i.e., ``constrained FIG''), the corresponding generations are easier to detect. We note that the models' f1 scores are low as they produce a lot of false negatives, i.e., predicting actual human-written texts to be machine-generated. However, we can observe that the addition of a small discourse vector significantly improves the performance.

This may imply that human authors tend to construct narratives with a higher prevalence of Joint relations, indicative of more evenly branching structures, in contrast to the patterns found in texts generated by LLMs. Another noteworthy observation is the heightened occurrence of the motif indexed at 28 (Temporal) with increasing text length to generate.
