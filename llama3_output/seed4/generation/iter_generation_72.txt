\subsection{Efficacy of 1-style control models}\label{sec:preference_first}
Our analysis of the generations of the fine-tuned Llama2 models reveals that the first-order effects of our training approach appear to be relatively well-controlled. See Table~\ref{tab:preference_first} for a summary of our results. We observe that the 1-style models are most successful at controlling for formality, the 2-style models, while the 3-style models make more substantial improvements in controlling for both dimensions. Notably, the 1-style models are also most effective in controlling for the most common style combinations. We also observe that the 1-style control model has the most stable generations according to the average perplexity values, following the original LLaMA2 generations as closely as the 2-style and 3-style models. For the results of all 1-style models, see Figure~\ref{fig:pref:first} in Appendix \ref{sec:appendix:pref}.
