\subsubsection{Motif Modality}
In our research, we explored various image modalities, including using image modality encoding, to represent node connections and graph structures. Our approach differentiates between the actual node content and the connections, using color to distinguish between the two. This color distinction is repeated across all modalities used in the encoding, including the image modality, to effectively convey the information regarding the node's connections. We also use a similar color scheme with the image modality, where red represents the node content and blue represents the connections. This color scheme is designed to be visually distinct and intuitive for human readers. We believe that this clarity in color representation is key to effectively communicating complex information to the reader.
