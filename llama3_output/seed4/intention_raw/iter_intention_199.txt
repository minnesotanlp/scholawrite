\section{Conclusion}
\rubric{Conclusion: }The use of large language models (LLMs) has become a standard practice in academia, and their use for organizing literature and composing Related Work sections is a relatively underexplored area. Our research has taken a step back to understand the nature of Related Work sections by examining the characteristics of artificial Related Work sections. We find that LLMs tend to not represent the relationships between the cited works well.
