They found that four of these types of LLM-generated data exhibit significant correlation with human data, while human data exhibits less correlation with the fifth type (instruction). This suggests that LLMs may have a more superficial approach to certain tasks than humans. 

In this paper, we examine whether LLM-generated task annotations fail to retain minority opinions and skew towards majority labels in various subjective and objective tasks and how this bias is amplified with further training.
