By employing a collaborative framework, LLMs are encouraged to refine their responses and better mirror the varied responses observed in human-generated texts. The approach involves designing a special prompt that introduces human-like variability in the LLM's outputs, simulating the creative process of multiple human authors. Moreover, it incorporates an adversarial learning component to more effectively incorporate new human opinions, thereby ensuring that the LLM can adapt and learn from the feedback of multiple human annotators. In this paper, we also explore the side-effects of the LLMs trained with this approach. The experiments conducted indicate that while the LLMs are able to generate more diverse responses, there is a notable elevation in  and unintended  consequences, such as instances of factual inaccuracies, where factuality is used as a side effect to achieve the desired effect.
