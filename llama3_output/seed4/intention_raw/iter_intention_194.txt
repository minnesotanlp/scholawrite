adjusts the linguistic conditions of sentences, particularly regarding formality, spontaneity, and depth of reasoning, aiming to align LLM-generated data with human characteristics. It does this through the use of "prompt words" or "constrained prompts," which are phrases or sentences that serve as guidelines for the model rather than actual instructions. For example, instead of saying "I love [verb] [object], it's great!" and generating a full paragraph, the model might say, "I love [verb] [object], and it's great!" or "I love [verb] [object], and it will be great!" The intention is to make the generated text more human-like by incorporating elements of the human's emotions or expectations into the prompt. However, this technique has a limited scope; it only influences the specified elements' style without assessing the overall quality or coherence of the generated text. When evaluating the performance of models fine-tuned on such data, we do not assess surface-level aspects, as the focus is on the general coherence and effectiveness in the data, rather than the surface-level formality.
