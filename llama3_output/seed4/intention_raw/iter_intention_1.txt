\subsection{Second Order Effects}\label{sec:preference-second}

\paragraph{Hypothesis} We examine if there is a discrepancy in the distribution between human and LLM preferences augmented by training a language model classifier on each human and LLM preference, which may cause a misalignment between them. 

\paragraph{Experiment Setup} We train RoBERTa \cite{liu2019roberta} on each of the human or machine evaluations from two different preference datasets, as a reward model. We first train four different reward models from each of the two datasets and then run on machine and human preferences. For each training session, we run RoBERTa for 5-10 epochs. 

\paragraph{Metrics} To get representations of the training distribution, we graph the logit versus probability of each class on test sets during inference, and we compare them between in-distribution sets (i.e., \textsc{Cobbler}-human vs \textsc{Cobbler}-machine, \textsc{p2c}-human vs \textsc{p2c}-machine).

We view in Table \ref{tab:preference:human} and \ref{tab:preference:machine} the validation accuracy from the human and machine preferences as well as their loss. From each of the train models, we also get the validation accuracy and loss on the validation set of the other annotations, which we see distinctly are lower with higher loss.
