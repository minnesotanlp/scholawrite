All three datasets already contain human and LLM preferences that are publicly available. 
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep,topsep=5pt,leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Instruction}: Our first-order data type is designed to be used for fine-tuning LLMs. It contains 25,000 instruction pairs with their respective references. The instruction pairs consist of a question or directive and its reference, which can be a human answer or a general guideline for the task. The purpose is to align the LLM with human reasoning \textit{instructions} are used to refine the performance of LLMs, particularly for understanding and executing complex instructions. 
\end{itemize}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep,topsep=5pt,leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Preference}: \textsc{CoBBLEr} \cite{koo2023benchmarking} contains preferences from humans and GPT-3 on 5,000 pairs of sentences designed for the sentiment classification task. We used all of these 5,000 samples for our first-order effect analysis. \textsc{GPT-4-LLM} \cite{peng2023instruction} consists of 24,000 samples of instructions generated by LLMs, paired with their reference human answers. We used all of these 24,000 samples for our second-order effect analysis as well. 
\end{itemize}
