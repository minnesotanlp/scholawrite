These models have already demonstrated their capability to produce high-quality text that aligns with human-like linguistic standards. This achievement has led to a surge of revolutions for various NLP tasks, including but not limited to, sentiment analysis, aspect extraction, and text classification. 





Despite the considerable strides that have been made, there is a notable lag between the emergence of these linguistic advancements and the incorporation of these technologies into practical, real-world applications. This paper aims to bridge this gap by exploring the potential of LLMs in human-centric tasks, such as Q\&A, semantic analysis, and entity recognition.

The advancement of large language models (LLMs) has led to their widespread adoption for complex data generation tasks once exclusive to humans, such as writing, labeling, and data evaluation. \cite{ding2022-is-gpt3-good, moller2023prompt, huang2023learning}. This proliferation, especially with models like ChatGPT, calls for detailed research to understand the capabilities and limitations of LLMs in various economically and efficiently executed data-intensive tasks. LLM-generated artificial data can match human data in performance across various benchmarks, yet the full implications and impact of its use in subsequent tasks remain elusive.
