\noindent \textit{Variation Analysis}
Our analysis as shown in Figure \ref{fig:variation_comparison}, reveals that the ChatGPT model consistently shows less variation in its responses to subjective tasks than humans, suggesting it oversimplifies these tasks or possesses strong predictive confidence. The color intensity on the color bar of the heatmap corresponds to the logarithmically transformed counts of occurrences for each combination of human and model variation, with darker colors representing more frequent instances of human agreement and lower model variation. Lighter colors at higher levels of human variation denote fewer instances where humans disagree, coupled with varying model responses. Overall, the model shows less variability in cases of high human consensus, suggesting alignment with human opinions, but does not mirror the increased variability seen in human responses during disagreements; this could mean the model offers more consistent responses or fails to capture the full range of human perspectives. Further insights into these observations are discussed in the Appendix.

We further qualitatively analyze text instances where there is significant human variation in labels but no model variation and where the minority label is not the same as the human minority label. In these cases, we observe clear evidence of {age}, {gender}, {political}, and  {race/religion bias}. This could be because minorities are underrepresented in the model's training data.
