\paragraph{Experiment Setup} We use LLMs as annotators to generate labels for the tasks of datasets described in Section \ref{sec:task-descriptions}. The LLMs we chose are all open-sourced or commercially available, instruction-tuned TRLs: Vicuna \cite{vicuna2023}, Koala \cite{koala_blogpost_2023}, Baize \cite{xu2023baize}, GPT-3.5-Turbo \cite{brown2020language}, and LLaMA 2\cite{touvron2023llama}. We create LLM task labels in two setups, \textit{intra-model} and \textit{inter-model}, and compare those generated labels against the original human annotations.

%In the \textit{intra-model} set-up, we group LLM ``annotators'' according to their underlying architectures and training methodologies, but differentiate between them using different temperature and top-p combinations. This results in a collection of models with different response patterns, simulating how a group of humans may respond differently from each other. 

%We use temperatures 0.3 and 0.8 and top-p values of 0.25 and 0.75 for 4 variations, in addition to the default 1.0 and 1.0 combination for 5 settings total. Thus, an intra-model setup for the model Vicuna would yield 5 different labels, each from a different combination of hyperparameters. When prompting the models, we extracted snippets of task descriptions from the original paper and included them in our instructions to the models, along with the original label names. We asked the LLM to respond as Master Turk workers chosen from a diverse group of people. Specific examples of our prompts are in the Appendix \ref{sec:appendix}.
