-tagged data is problematic as it significantly under-represents minority opinions. The under-representation of minority opinions in LLM-generated data and the potential for skewing this data towards majority viewpoints has been noted in various studies \cite{li2023synthetic, chan2023chateval, chen2023exploring}. Previous works have addressed this by incorporating Bayesian methods and utilizing annotation disagreements in model training \cite{plank2014learning}. 

The growing reliance on artificially generated data also poses a risk of data contamination \cite{shumailov2023curse}. This occurs when the use of LLM-generated data in training causes a degradation in the quality and diversity of the data. This contamination can then be amplified when such data is used in subsequent training cycles. 

\anonymous{Another crucial concern in the deployment of LLMs is their well-documented tendency to perpetuate harmful stereotypes and biases against marginalized groups \cite{hosseini-etal-2023-empirical,gallegos2023bias,wald2023exposing,pmlr-v139-liang21}. Studies have shown that LLMs encode biases against people with disabilities \cite{liang2023encouraging}, African-American English speakers and minority identity mentions \cite{xu-etal-2021-detoxifying}, individuals with stigmatized conditions \cite{10.1145/3597307.3593679}, LGBTQIA+ individuals \cite{nozza-etal-2022-measuring}, Muslims \cite{Abid2021}, minoritized women \cite{cheng-etal-2023-marked}, caste \cite{khandelwal2023casteist}, nationality \cite{narayanan-venkit-etal-2023-nationality}, and non-English languages \cite{ramesh-etal-2023-fairness,talat-etal-2022-reap}. Additionally, the problem of model collapse, where models trained on their own generated data start forgetting the true underlying data distribution \cite{shumailov2023curse}, exacerbates this issue. The concern is that the degenerative process could magnify existing biases and stereotypes, thereby reducing their ability to capture the diversity and complexity of human data.}

\anonymous{These concerns motivate our exploration of the artifactuality of LLM-generated data. Our goal is not to conduct an exhaustive empirical evaluation of LLM-generated data, but rather to perform light stress testing to uncover some artifacts that may indicate potential biases or tendencies towards model collapse. This approach allows us to explore the characteristics of synthetic data and identify areas that may require more detailed investigation. In exploring these artifacts, we aim to contribute preliminary insights into the nature of LLM-generated data, particularly in how it compares to human data, as well as to express the importance of vigilance and responsible practices in dataset creation and LLM development, especially as these technologies increasingly intersect with critical social issues.}

We prioritize breadth, covering five different data types (examples of each shown in Figure \ref{fig:figure1}), rather than depth, to understand overarching themes in synthetic data.
