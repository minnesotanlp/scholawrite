Facets 1, 2, and 3 refer to the three-style control experiments, while Facets 4-6 refer to the three-style control experiments including sentiment and formality. In both cases, the three-style combinations are more difficult to learn than the two-style combinations, and the difficulties can be attributed to the small size of the signal from one style.

\subsection{Efficacy of 1-style models}
Our single-style models achieve the desired style a majority of the time ($78.55\%$ for positive, $64.05\%$ for negative, $87.50\%$ for formal, and $76.5\%$ for informal). See results in Figure~\ref{fig:single-style}, Table~\ref{tab:precision_results}. Importantly, the original model generations lean toward being positive and informal.
