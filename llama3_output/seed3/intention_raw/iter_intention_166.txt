\item \textbf{LLMs have no inherent limitations regarding the types of tasks they are applied to. } 
These concerns motivate our exploration of the task diversity in synthetically created instruction datasets and the effects of the different types of errors present in these datasets.
\item \textbf{The quality and diversity of synthetic instruction datasets are unclear. } 
\textit{Instruction prompts in LLM-generated data come in various forms, and the extent to which these prompts adhere to the guidelines in the original task descriptions is unclear.} 
\textit{Moreover, the high error rates in instructions, as reported in studies like Unnatural Instructions and Self-Instruct, raise concerns about the reliability of the models trained on these datasets.} 
\textit{If the trends observed in these datasets hold true, it means that the models would be struggling to generate diverse and accurate instructions.} 
\textit{Therefore, the first issue is the high error rates in the generated instructions.} 
\textit{The second issue is the lack of diversity in the datasets against the types of tasks and domains.} 
\textit{The third issue is the fact that these datasets are not thoroughly examined for errors.} 
\end{itemize}
