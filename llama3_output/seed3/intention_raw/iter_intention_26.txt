\textbf{Result Analysis}

\textbf{Comparison of majority and minority match score across subjective datasets :} Considering the inter-model setup, we can see that in Figure \ref{fig:maj_match}, we observe that across all the subjective datasets, human and model majority match accuracy is highest predominantly for GPT3.5 Turbo, except in the case of GHC, for which Koala also has a surprisingly high majority match accuracy. SChem5Labels shows a high majority match accuracy with nearly all the datasets except llama2, implying that this is probably one of the easier subjective tasks. While the majority match accuracy is high,  the minority match accuracy is also lowest for GPT3.5 Turbo. This shows that with increased task subjectivity, the LLM tends to skew strongly towards the majority opinion, showing that minority opinions are not preserved in LLM-generated task labels.\\
