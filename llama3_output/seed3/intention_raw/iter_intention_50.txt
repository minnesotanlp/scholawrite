First, we check how the different generation settings affect the detection performance (Table \ref{tab:overall_f1_ten_page_stories}). As expected the detection model performs better with longer generated texts (i.e., 1, 3, and 5 paragraphs). Also, when the first and last sentences of the human-written texts are additionally provided in the prompt (i.e., ``constrained FIG''), the corresponding generations are easier to detect. We note that the models' f1 scores are low as they produce a lot of false negatives, i.e., predicting actual human-written texts to be machine-generated. However, we can observe that the addition of a small discourse vector significantly improves the performance.
