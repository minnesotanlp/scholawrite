\subsubsection*{Behavior of Uncontrolled Styles}
We examine how generations from the style-controlled models differ from the original model with respect to the \textit{uncontrolled} styles.

Certain textual styles are highly correlated, e.g. \textit{positive} and \textit{joy}. As expected, models with the target style positive show a corresponding increase in the joy of their generations. However, we also note that the informal model shows an increase in joy comparable to the positive models. To find whether there is a relationship between informality and positivity, we checked the original data's full texts and the base model's generations for correlations between positivity and informality. We found they were not highly correlated (Pearson's $r = 0.1$, $p < 0.05$; this also replicates earlier results from \cite{kang2021style}). This implies that this result cannot be attributed to any inherent association between the styles, and it is possible that steering generations towards a target style may impact the prevalence of non-target styles as well.
