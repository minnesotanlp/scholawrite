Through our study, we find that artificial free text data uses significant synthetic speech patterns that are not present in human-written text. Artificial free text data also demonstrates less variation in lower-level textual attributes than human data, and this is likely due to the difficulty of generating texts that are similar to human-written text. 

To mitigate the effect of these artifacts, we suggest enhancing data quality and diversity by incorporating domain-specific information and examples into LLM training, in order to expose LLMs to a broader range of perspectives. We also recommend that LLM-generated free text data should be used in a more light-weight manner, such as use as a prompt for other tasks, rather than a replacement for human data.
