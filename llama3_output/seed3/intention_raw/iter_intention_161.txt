\subsection{Imbalance in Style Combinations}
We hypothesize that style combinations that occur more frequently in natural text are easier for generative models to learn. Because style combinations that are rare in natural text are more difficult to learn, rare style combinations may be more difficult for generative models to learn than style combinations that are more common. We can see in the  that style combinations that are more common in the human texts are easier for the LLM to learn. This suggests that LLMs may be able to learn more easily the style combinations that are more common in the human texts.
