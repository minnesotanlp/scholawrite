Credentialing  LLMs with citation information and using this data for authorship detection. 

We observed that a greater number of citations, often discussed in the context of related work, seem to be a factor when determining the suitability of an LLM-generated text. This suggests a potential relationship between the use of citation-related information and the quality of LLM-generated texts. However, it is also important to consider that citation  and the extent to which the context of the related work is being properly addressed. Future work could explore these ideas.

\subsection{Data}
We used the \textsc{Pubmed} dataset \cite{sen2008collective}, which consists of scientific journals collected from the PubMed database. The data representation used in our study includes the title, abstract, introduction, related work, conclusion, and citations from the LLM-assisted and GPT-4-generated texts. The title, abstract, introduction, and conclusion represent the context of the original related work, while the related work section represents the original related work section. Each citation in the citation list includes its bibliographic metadata, including title, abstract, authors, year, and a URL to its PubMed entry.
