\section{Introduction}
\rubric{Hook: } The rapid acceleration of knowledge creation is a defining feature of modern research. Between 1952 and 2020, the corpus of scientific literature has expanded at a growth rate of 5.08\%, doubling every 14 years \cite{Bornmann2021}. Some fields are growing even more rapidly; \citet{Krenn2023} estimate a doubling rate of almost two years for machine learning and artificial intelligence papers between 2022 and 2023. Global crises are also associated with rapid literature growth. Climate change publication has experienced a doubling rate of every 5-6 years from 1980 to 2014 \cite{10.1371/journal.pone.0160393}, and \citet{Chen_Allot_Leaman_Wei_Aghaarabi_Guerrerio_Xu_Lu_2022} reported a COVID-19 literature growth rate of approximately 10,000 articles per month from the Spring of 2020 to the Summer of 2022.

The act of writing is a dynamic aspect of academic inquiry, not a procedural task to complete at the end of the research process. The writing process works in a feedback loop, allowing the writer to refine, solidify, or even challenge their understanding; it informs the writer almost as much as it does the reader. Through the practice of writing, researchers not only contribute to the body of scientific knowledge, but also develop their own cognitive and analytical skills.
