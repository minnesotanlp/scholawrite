Deviating from this fact, we intentionally set a much larger number of generations to explore (3,000), a much smaller batch size (50) and do not use the GradWt fine-tuning method. Our goal is to understand the exploration strategy of GPT-4, rather than its fine-tuned performance. We use the default hyperparameters for GPT-4, i.e., the default KL-related hyperparameters. We do not use the default hyperparameters for the inner and outer models because we found that using the same hyperparameters for the inner and outer models would not help the exploration of the system. We use the temperature 0.3 and top-p values of 0.25 and 0.75 for 3,000 generations each. We keep the default hyperparameters for RLHF generation, i.e., temperature 0.03 and top-p value of 0.25 for 50 generations. We do not use the GradWt fine-tuning method because we are interested in understanding the baseline performance of GPT-4 without the fine-tuned language model. We consider the generations of the 3,000 generations for each of the three inner models (LLaMA, GPT-4, GPT-4-RL) in two separate hyperparameter searches to understand how the resulting models would perform.
