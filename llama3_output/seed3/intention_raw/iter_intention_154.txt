\subsection{Imbalance in Style Combinations}\label{sec:side_effects}
We hypothesize that rare style combinations are more difficult to learn, as style combination imbalances can cause difficulty for style transfer \cite{das2023balancing}.
% \citet{das2023balancing} addresses such style imbalance problems by balancing their combinatory distributions from the training data. %\anonymous{Added this. Please double check. }
We approach understanding the relative frequency of the possible style combinations from two perspectives: frequency in (a) the LLM's responses to the prompts, and (b) the full human texts from the evaluation dataset. In both cases, formal and negative is the least common combination, and formal and positive is the most common (see Figure~\ref{fig:before-and-after}). We observe that while our proposed method's efficacy varies with the specific style combinations, our fine-tuned models are still able to generate substantially higher portions of difficult style combinations than the pre-trained model.
