To explore LLM-generated instruction datasets, we annotated 200 samples each of Unnatural Instructions, Self-Instruct, Alpaca-Cleaned, and GPT-4-LLM (a total of 800 samples). Our annotation process focused on whether the instruction takes the form of closed versus open questions and answering; and the prevalence of errors and distribution of error types. Our \textsc{Closed-QA} samples are defined as tasks with specific, objective answers, using given input or specific criteria. On the other hand, \textsc{Open-QA} samples are defined as more exploratory, subjective, or creative tasks. They typically involve broader questions or tasks with multiple correct answers or approaches.

We also performed an error analysis on all 800 samples. For this analysis, we considered four error labels: (1) \textsc{Incomprehensible Instruction} (i.e., impossible to follow based on the provided input); (2) \textsc{Inconsistent Input} (i.e., the input does not support or contradicts the instruction); (3) \textsc{Incorrect Output} (i.e., the output is an incorrect solution given the instruction and input); and (4) \textsc{Inconsistent Output} (i.e., the output is formally inconsistent with the expected task description). Labels one through three are the same as the error labels created by \citet{honovich2022unnatural} and \citet{wang2023selfinstruct}.
