\section{Conclusion}
\anonymous{ tie back to motivation, talk about the artificial future and need to find more artifacts, investigate these }

In the era of overflowing LLM-generated data for all kinds of use cases, researchers found a huge need to gain a broad understanding of those artificial data, particularly in terms of how these data diverge from human-annotated counterparts. Using various types of stress methods, we identified artifacts across five different types of LLM-generated data, which are also amplified if used as training data for another LLM. However, our suggestions to minimize such disparity will increase the reliability of LLM-generated data as sources of model development and thus promote better research implications under responsible and ethical considerations. 

While our study examined several types of LLM-generated data, there could be more hidden artifacts in such data types that cannot be easily captured by our stress testing methods. We put this as a future topic for the NLP community.
