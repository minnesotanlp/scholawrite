\subsection{Imbalance in Style Combinations}\label{sec:combinations}
We hypothesize that rarer style combinations are more difficult to learn.
\citet{das2023balancing} addresses such style imbalance problems by balancing their combinatory distributions from the training data. %\anonymous{Added this. Please double check. }
Similarly, we approach understanding the relative frequency of the possible style combinations from two perspectives: frequency in the human texts in the datasets and frequency in the baseline generations in the datasets. In both cases, formal and negative is the least common combination, and formal and positive is the most common (see Figure~\ref{fig:before-and-after}). We observe that while our proposed method's efficacy varies with the specific target style combination, it consistently produces generations of both target styles relative to the pre-trained model.
% \anonymous{Again, we are selling our product (i.e., proposed method) here, so please explicitly say that our proposed method helps a lot better control (even difficult) combination of styles, compared to the baselines, althoguh their natural difficulty still exists.}
