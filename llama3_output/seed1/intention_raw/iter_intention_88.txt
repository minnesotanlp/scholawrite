\subsection{Mitigation Suggestions}\label{sec:instruct-mitigation}
Given the limitations we discovered in the synthetic instruction datasets and their impact on model performance, we propose several mitigation strategies when using synthetic instructions. We suggest that researchers first closely inspect the data resource, focusing on errors in the instructions. Researchers may also want to perform automatic preprocessing to clean the data by checking the consistency of the inputs and outputs with the instructions.

The reader may want to consider generating their own data following the work by \cite{Lee2023EnsembleInstructGI}, who make a similar observation to ours regarding the difference between Open-QA and Closed-QA instructions (but categorize samples into whether they have an input). They used In-Context Learning demonstrations, and use ensembling to select higher quality synthetic instructions.

We also recommend evaluating whether the dataset is truly diverse; \cite{Wang2023HowFC} observe that instruction-tuned models vary widely, but perform better when the fine-tuning data contains diverse skills and domains. 

To promote diversity in task categories, we would suggest to use diversity-promoting techniques such as criteria-based prompting \cite{hayati2023far}.
