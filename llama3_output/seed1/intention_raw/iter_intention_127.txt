While synthetic data has become an increasingly important component of AI and machine learning (ML) research, the characteristics and impacts of synthetic data remain vastly understudied. 

\textbf{The issue: } Synthetic data generated by LLMs is notoriously difficult to distinguish from human data. This ambiguity is due to LLMs generating data in a "true" human-like manner, i.e., by simulating human experiences, opinions, and interactions. This similarity leads LLMs to be considered "the next best thing to human data" (Hernández-Andrés et al., 2022), yet little work has been done to comprehensively understand the nature of this data. 


\textbf{Our contribution: } Our research aims to systematically identify the characteristics of synthetic data, using various stress tests to analyze the immediate imperfections in the data (what we call 1st order effect) and the potential amplification of these imperfections when such data is used to train new models (2nd order effects). We hypothesize that the artifact amplifies as we go to higher-order effects, which could be potentially catastrophic to downstream applications. 

\textbf{What we do} - We use various forms of stress testing to analyze the 1st order effects of synthetic data. We stress test synthetic data by analyzing the  data to identify the most common errors and the extent to which these errors affect the data. We also observe the 2nd order effects, which further we identify the artifacts amplified in the training of the models on synthetic data.
