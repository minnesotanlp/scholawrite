\section{Overall Discussion}
\label{sec:sec9}

Figure \ref{fig:figure2} demonstrates the variety of techniques applied for stress testing artificial data, as detailed in earlier sections, while Table \ref{tab:stress_testing_overview} provides an overview of the methodologies employed for each data type. Notably, approaches like \textit{distributional difference} prove effective for contrasting almost all categories of LLM-generated data with human-generated data. Methods such as \textit{label flipping} and \textit{correlation analysis} are widely utilized across numerous data types. Except for Simulation data, which is still in the nascent stages of exploration and requires more first-order analysis, second-order effect analysis is conducted for all data types.

\noindent \textbf{Main findings}
\textbf{Main findings}
\textit{First order effects}
The five types of artificial data we examine each have a first order effect related to the majority of the data type
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep,topsep=5pt,leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Free-Form Text} artifical ``high-level'' attributes such as \textit{``formality''} and \textit{``discourse structure''} are not present in texts generated by LLMs. 
    \item \textbf{Task Labels} LLMs are deficient in accurately applying textual  \textit{``s} and \textit{``social bias''), with a tendency to over-represent majority opinions and dismiss minority viewpoints.
    \item \textbf{Instruction prompts} LLMs are prone to producing errors in instruction-tuned tasks, with a high rate of misclassification and poor performance in closed questions, especially those requiring specific knowledge or precise answers.
    \item \textbf{Preference} LLMs show a strong tendency to show locality bias or be sensitive to local linguistic cues of texts that are more salient than humans when judging their preferences.
    \item \textbf{Simulation} LLM agents frequently lose track of their assigned roles and stray from the topic of conversation, often after apologizing for confusion, suggesting their inability to navigate challenging conversational shifts adeptly.
\end{itemize}
