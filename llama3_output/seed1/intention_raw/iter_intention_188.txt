Our analysis shows a divide in our four synthetic datasets in terms of error rates, with \textsc{Unnatural Instructions} and \textsc{Self-Instruct} showing a higher error rate compared to \textsc{Cleaned Alpaca} and \textsc{GPT-4-LLM}. This may be due to the LLMs' difficulty in following instructions when they encounter unknown scenarios. 

\paragraph{Second Order Experiment}
We hypothesize that the effect of errors in the instructions will be amplified when the LLM is tasked with generating a  \textit{task label} classification, compared to when the LLM is only exposed to the output of the task label.

\noindent \textit{Variation Analysis} In our analysis in Figure \ref{fig:variation_comparison}, we find that the ChatGPT model exhibits less variation in its responses to subjective tasks than humans, which might indicate an oversimplification of these tasks or high predictive confidence. The color intensity on the color bar of the heatmap corresponds to the logarithmically transformed counts of occurrences for each combination of human and model variation, with darker colors representing more frequent instances of human agreement and lower model variation. Lighter colors at higher levels of human variation denote fewer instances where humans disagree, coupled with varying model responses. Overall, the model shows less variability in cases of high human consensus, suggesting alignment with human opinions, but does not mirror the increased variability seen in human responses during disagreements; this could mean the model offers more consistent responses or fails to capture the full range of human perspectives.
