This discovery is substantiated by the observation that machine-generated texts exhibit lower variability in high-level textual attributes (such as irony and metaphor) compared to their human-authored counterparts. This suggests that while machines can generate texts that align with the given social dimension labels, the resulting texts often show a significantly different spread of high-level textual attributes.


Our findings indicate that the resulting texts from LLMs tend to be standardized and follow a more scripted way of thinking rather than being truly dynamic and unique like human-authored texts.
