\subsubsection{\textsc{CoBBLEr}}\label{sec:preference-data-cobbler}
In a more \textit{open-ended} exploration, we deploy a \textsc{RoBERTa-base} model \citep{liu2019roberta} proficient in both \textit{sentiment} and \textit{formality} classification. 
Our investigation examines the potential of \textsc{CoBBLEr} dataset, featuring conversations between two LLM agents named \textit{Assistant} and \textit{User}. 
The \textsc{CoBBLEr} dataset is a collection of dialogues on 25,000 conversations between two LLM agents. These conversations are designed to explore the dynamics of different role assignments of the agents. 
The conversation begins with Assistant's initial message as a "prompt," and then User responds with their own "prompt." 
We first generate 200 conversations between two assistants and then have a conversation between an assistant and a user. 
In this investigation, we are interested in whether the personas of the agents change during the conversation. 
We have two versions of generations: \textit{unconstrained} and \textit{constrained}. 
In the \textit{unconstrained} generation, we instruct the LLM to produce a generation based on the conversation between two agents without considering the personas of the agents. 
For example, the instruction to be used for generations is as follows: \textit{Feelyou may be thinking of a situation in which someone is wearing a big smile on their face and their teeth are slightly aligned.} 
This type of generation involves allowing the LLM to generate a generation based on the prompt without considering the personas of the agents. 
In the \textit{constrained} generation, we instruct the LLM to produce a generation based on the conversation between two agents considering their personas. 
For example, the instruction to be used for generations is as follows: \textit{Feelyou may be thinking of a situation in which someone is wearing a big smile on their face and their teeth are slightly aligned.} 
% \textbf{Constrained }For the generation to be considered constrained, the first four words of the prompt must be followed by the last four words.
