These synthetic datasets often mirror the styles and outputs of their parent LLM, with a lack of comprehensive documentation about what task types are included. In contrast, human-crafted datasets \cite{2020unifiedqa, muennighoff2022crosslingual,lai2023chatgpt, sanh2021multitask, mishra2021natural, supernaturalinstructions, DatabricksBlog2023DollyV2} show a wider range of distinct task types. This limitation is further exacerbated by the LLMs' focus on superficial patterns and output formats \citep{kung-peng-2023-models}, which can lead to a narrow scope of learning and adaptability in handling diverse real-world scenarios. Additionally, the high error rates in instructions generated by LLMs raise concerns about the reliability and accuracy of the models trained on these datasets (\citet{honovich2022unnatural} and \citet{wang2023selfinstruct} both report error rates close to 50\% for their respective instruction datasets).
