\subsection{Datasets}\label{sec:free-data}

We utilize the \textsc{HC3} \cite{guo2023close}, \textsc{Scarecrow} \cite{dou-etal-2022-gpt}, \textsc{Deepfake} \cite{li2023deepfake}, and \textsc{Workers} \cite{moller2023prompt}. 
\paragraph{Rationale} These datasets include generated texts from both humans and LLMs in a pairwise format, which enables us to compare the difference between human-written and LLM-generated texts. 


% despite its small size as \textsc{HC3} contains questions and corresponding answers from both humans and LLMs. We filtered the data samples by a length ratio of 0.7 so that the remaining 5,529 paired documents from the five domains (finance, medicine, open QA, Reddit ELI5, and Wiki CS AI) are of similar length to each other. The \textsc{Scarecrow} dataset \citep{dou-etal-2022-gpt} that consists of 13K annotations of 1.3K human-authored and machine-generated paragraphs of English language news is also used in full. The \textsc{Deepfake} dataset \citep{li2023deepfake} covers a diverse range of paired human-machine text. This dataset sources machine text from several LLMs with different prompting strategies, some of which include a paired human text in the prompt. The \textsc{Workers} dataset \cite{moller2023prompt} has a prompting strategy that includes the full human text in the prompt as an example. For all datasets, to ensure data quality, we applied filtering criteria to remove unmatched samples and for discourse analysis, we removed samples with significant variations in text length.
