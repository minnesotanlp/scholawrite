\noindent
In our research, we explore the use of LLM-generated artificial data and its effects when used in the training pipeline, known as \textit{artificial data artifacts}. LLMs are increasingly employed to create a variety of outputs, including free text, simulated dialogues, annotations, instruction prompts, and evaluation. As these forms of LLM-generated data are often intertwined in their application, they exert mutual influence on each other and raise significant concerns about the quality and diversity of the artificial data incorporated into training cycles, leading to a \textit{artificial data ecosystem}. 

We hypothesize that LLM-generated data, by its nature, is difficult to distinguish from human data \cite{ding2022-is-gpt3-good, moller2023prompt, huang2023learning}, particularly in 3+1 style tasks, and this may cause a 3+1 style task, leading to decreased performance and increased errors.  We also note that the nature of LLM-generated data and its effects on downstream tasks are not fully understood.
